Data Scientist

Paris, France

My name is Ndeye Maguette Mbaye, and I am a Ph.D. graduate in Bioinformatics from Mines Paris PSL in Paris, France, under the supervision of Dr. Chloé-Agathe AZENCOTT. My ph.D. was conducted at the Center for Computational Biology (CBIO) of ARMINES/Mines Paris PSL, Institut Curie and INSERM. I am also an alumni from the The Marie Curie Innovative Training Network entitled “Machine Learning Frontiers in Precision Medicine”, which brings together early stage researchers working on the similar themes around ML and statistical analysis for medical data. My reseach focused on integrating and analyzing multimodal Electronic Health Records (EHR), which included text, biological, and clinical data from a breast cancer cohort in the Institut Curie. I utilized a range of machine learning (ML) techniques, including classical and more advanced models like transformers, to extract meaningful insights and improve the predictive power of clinical data analysis. I am skilled in applying ML/DL and bioinformatics methodologies to tackle complex biomedical problems and drive advancements in personalized medicine.

Research Projects:

Tabular BEHRT

I developed Tabular BEHRT which is a readaption of a deep neural sequence transduction for electronical health records called BEHRT using as input data the sequential information through the patient journey. The BEHRT model is inspired by one the most powerful transformer-based model in Natural Language Processing: BERT. Tabular BEHRT consists of using BERT-like architecture on a readaptation of medical events into a sequential format.

More informations are available on the preprint here

Text BEHRT

Text BEHRT explores the integration of free text medical reports with advanced deep learning methods such as transformers for DFS status prediction. I developed Text BEHRT, a transformer-based models that use the sequence of free text reports through the patient journey, to assess its efficacity in DFS status prediction. As the previous chapter, Text-BEHRT is inspired by the BERT model. In this chapter, I will present the whole pipeline that: (i) processes french free-text medical reports into valuable information, (ii) readapt them into sequences of free text trajectories and that (iii) models this information through Text-BEHRT.

More informations are available on the preprint here

M-BEHRT

Electronic Health Records contains a wealth of information accross various modalities, including structured data and unstructured data. The integration of multimodal data can capture the complexity of patient health status. After Tabular BEHRT that provides a comprehensive view of structured EHR information for Disease Free Survival (DFS) status prediction, Text BEHRT that used the full spectrum of information avalaible in free-text medical notes, we aggregate these two models through a Multimodal transformer-based model called M-BEHRT (Multimodal-BEHRT).

EUvsVIRUS hackathon

This challenge was about building tools that could help solving COVID-19 related problems. Our team developed a tool called HospiNet to simulate the number of patients in ICY il all hospitals in France and how the transfer between hospitals can be optimized to ensure a better distribution.
